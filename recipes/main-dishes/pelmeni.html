Pelmeni

Recipe source(s): Allrecipes

Description

Pelmeni are Russian tiny meat-filled dumplings. Making them by hand with parents is a classic family pastime during the cold and dark winter evenings for many East Slavs. Or, at least, it used to be.

These dumplings are a common convenience food. Large batches can be frozen and quickly boiled or fried on demand.

Requirements

Equipment
  • Liquid measuring cup.
  • Regular cup.
  • Meat grinder (preferably).
  • Large pot.
  • Large bowl.
  • Medium bowl.
  • Rolling pin.
  • Baking sheet.
  • Dish towel.
  • Fork.
  • Slotted spoon.
Ingredients

Dough:

  • 1 large egg.
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil.
  • 1 teaspoon salt.
  • ¾ cup warm water, or more as needed.
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour.
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour for dusting.

Filling:

  • 18 ounces ground beef.
    • You can use a mixture of 1/2 ground pork and 1/2 ground beef.
    • You can also use ground turkey.
  • 1 small onion, chopped.
  • 1 ½ tablespoons ice-cold water.
  • 1 tablespoon coarse salt.
  • Freshly ground pepper to taste.

Steps

  1. Make dough:
    1. Combine egg, oil, and salt in a liquid measuring cup; add enough warm water to fill to 1 cup.
    2. Pour into a large bowl and knead in 3 cups flour until smooth and elastic.
      • You can make the dough in a Kitchen Aid; mix until the dough begins to form a ball.
    3. Cover with a dish towel and let dough rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, make filling:
    1. Combine ground beef, onion, water, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl.
      • If you're not a fan of raw onion, you can sauté the onion in butter over medium heat for 5 to 7 minutes before using it in the filling.
    2. Mix thoroughly by hand or using a fork.
      • You can make the meat filling by processing the filling ingredients through a meat grinder. Only use a blender or food processor if you have a special setting for meat.
    3. Set aside.
  3. Dust a baking sheet lightly with 1 tablespoon flour; set aside.
  4. Make the dumplings:
    1. Roll out a portion of dough very thinly on a lightly floured surface; keep remaining dough covered with a dish towel to avoid drying out.
    2. Cut out 2 1/2-inch rounds with a cookie cutter.
    3. Place about 1 teaspoon filling on one side of each dough circle.
    4. Fold dough over and seal the edges using your fingers, forming a crescent. Join the ends and pinch them together.
    5. Place on the prepared baking sheet. Repeat with remaining dough and filling.
    6. Freeze pelmeni for 30 minutes to prevent them from sticking together.
  5. Cook them:
    1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a simmer.
    2. Working in batches, cook frozen pelmeni in simmering water until meat is cooked through and pelmeni float to the top, about 5 minutes.
    3. Continue cooking for 5 more minutes.
    4. Transfer to serving plates using a slotted spoon.

How to fry

Melt butter or bacon grease in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sauté frozen pelmeni in hot butter until soft and meat is cooked through, just like a pierogi.
