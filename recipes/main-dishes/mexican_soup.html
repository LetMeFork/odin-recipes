Mexican Soup

Recipe source(s): Culinary Hill

Description

Put leftover chicken to work in this easy Mexican Chicken Soup recipe. It comes together fast, and it’s good for whatever ails you, even if it’s just a case of the Monday blues.

Requirements

Equipment
  • Large saucepan.
  • Knife.
  • Spoon.
Ingredients

For the soup:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil.
  • 1 medium onion chopped (about 2 cups).
  • 3 carrots peeled and chopped (about 1 cup).
  • 3 ribs celery chopped (about 1 cup).
  • 2 cloves garlic minced.
  • 6 cups chicken broth.
  • 1 (14 ounce) can crushed tomatoes undrained.
  • 1 jalapeño pepper seeded and minced.
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin.
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander.
  • 3 cups cooked chicken diced or shredded.
    • If you don’t have any leftover chicken on hand, learn how to poach chicken breasts (it’ll be ready by the time you need to add the chicken to the soup).
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro chopped (optional).
    • If you’re not a fan of cilantro, just leave this out entirely. Maybe sub a pinch of Mexican oregano.
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper.

For serving:

  • Shredded cheddar cheese.
  • Sour cream.
  • Avocado peeled, pitted, and diced.
  • Tortilla chips.

Steps

  1. In a large saucepan, heat olive oil over medium-high heat until shimmering.
  2. Add onion, carrots, and celery.
  3. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables have softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the garlic until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  5. Stir in in the broth, tomatoes and juice, jalapeño, cumin, and coriander.
  6. Bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer, uncovered, for 20 minutes. Stir in chicken and cilantro (if using). Season to taste (for example, 1 teaspoon salt and ½ teaspoon pepper).
  7. Serve, offering, cheese, sour cream, avocado, and tortilla chips or your favorite toppings.

Storage

Store leftovers covered in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
