Bread Kvas

Recipe source(s): Natasha's Kitchen

Description

A simple, authentic bread kvas that doesn’t need a concentrate.

Requirements

Equipment
  • Giant stock pot / Two large pots.
  • Toaster.
  • Medium bowl.
  • Large spoon.
  • Lid / Plastic wrap.
  • Strainer / Cheese cloth.
  • Bottles (preferably plastic soda bottles since they are designed to hold pressurized drinks).
Ingredients
  • 2.5 gallons or 10 qt of water.
  • 1 lb or 9 slices of classic black, dark or rye bread.
  • 1 handful of raisins.
  • 1.8 lb (4 cups) of sugar.
  • 1.5 Tbsp of active dry yeast.
  • 3 large plastic soda bottles.

Steps

Day 1

Best if prepared in the evening:

  1. Fill giant stock pot with 2.5 gallons of water (or divide it into two large pots) and bring to a boil.
  2. While waiting, toast the bread slices twice on the darkest toaster setting.
    • Darker bread makes darker kvas.
    • Toast bread either outside or in your garage or your house will get smokey.
  3. When water starts to boil, remove the pot from heat.
  4. Add a handful of raisins and toasted bread to the pot, cover with the lid and let it stay overnight or at least 8 hours.

Day 2

  1. Carefully remove toasted bread and discard it.
  2. In a medium bowl, mix together 4 cups of sugar and 1.5 Tbsp of yeast, add them to kvas mixture and stir.
  3. Cover with plastic wrap or lid and leave the mixture on the counter for another 6 hours, stirring every couple hours.
  4. Discard floating raisins by scooping them up with a large spoon.
  5. Using strainer or cheese cloth, pour kvas into bottles, loosely cover with lid and refrigerate overnight.

The following day once the bottles are completely chilled, you can tighten the lid.

Enjoy!

Storage

This kvas lasts up to a week in the fridge (probably longer, but it might start tasting kinda strong). You’ll notice it loses sweetness daily as it stands.

Make sure to release the pressure from the bottles 1-2 times a day if an overinflating makes you nervous.

kvas only has up to 1% alcohol content (still probably not recommended for pregos). The longer it sits in the fridge, the more slightly “alcoholic” it gets, but it’s still considered non-alcoholic. I think it’s best after a full day in the fridge.
