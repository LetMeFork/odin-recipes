French Fries

Recipe source(s): The Cozy Cook

Description

Learn the secret to making the best homemade french fries from russet potatoes right at home! These are easy to fry or bake in the oven for a quick snack or side dish idea!

Requirements

Ingredients
  • 10 large russet potatoes, peeled and rinsed. Calculate about 2 large Russet potatoes per person.
    • Since they’re dense, they don’t contain as much water inside, which allows them to get extra crispy.
    • Waxy potatoes are the worst. These include red potatoes, fingerling, and new potatoes. They’re high in moisture, and will actually become hollow when fried as the water evaporates out.
  • Vegetable oil.
  • Salt.
  • Ketchup or fry sauce (optional).
Equipment
  • Knife / French Fry Cutter / anything else for slicing potatoes.
  • Paper towels.
  • Plate.
  • Deep fryer / Dutch oven.
  • Oil thermometer.
  • Slotted spoon.
  • Freezer bag (for refrigerating).

Steps

Want to know the secret on how to make French fries super crispy? Get rid of the starch by soaking the potatoes in cold water and remove moisture by cooking them twice!

Preparations

  1. Slice the potatoes into ½ inch thick sticks. Using a French fry cutter makes this much easier.
  2. Soak the potato slices in cold water for at least 1 hour, and up to overnight (the longer, the crispier the fries). Store them in the fridge while they soak.
  3. Rinse the fries twice with cold water.
  4. Lay the fries on paper towels and pat them completely dry.

Then choose your cooking method: baking or frying.

Method 1: Baking

  1. Toss with up to 1/2 cup vegetable oil and sprinkle with salt.
  2. Place the fries in a single layer on a baking sheet at 400° F / 200° C in the lower-third of the oven for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Remove, toss, place in a single layer, and bake for 10 more minutes or until brown and crispy.

Method 2: Frying

First round:

  1. Heat oil in a deep fryer or dutch oven to 300° F / 150° C.
    • This allows the middle of the potato to cook.
    • You’ll need to fry in about six batches, so begin with a quart of oil and add more as needed. Frying too many at once makes them less crispy.
  2. Add the potatoes to the oil with a slotted spoon and fry for about 5-6 minutes.
    • The oil should bubble lightly. We’re only heating the potatoes, we don’t want them to crisp yet.
  3. Place the cooked potatoes on a paper towel lined plate.
  4. Fry the rest of the remaining batches.

Second round:

  1. Increase the heat to 400° F / 200° C.
    • This allows the outside to become crispy and golden.
  2. Fry each batch a second time until they are crisp and golden-brown, about 5 minutes or so.
  3. Remove them and place on dry paper towels. Sprinkle with salt as soon as they come out.
  4. Serve with ketchup or fry sauce and enjoy!

Store and Reheat

  • Let any leftover fries cool completely.
  • Store them in a Freezer bag in the fridge, removing as much air from the bag as possible.
  • To reheat, bake at 350° F / 175° C for about 10 minutes. They reheat very well.
